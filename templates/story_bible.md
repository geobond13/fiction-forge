# Story Bible

> **Purpose**: This is the single source of truth for your novel's world, voice, and
> canon. AI writing assistants reference this file to maintain consistency across
> chapters. Every decision recorded here constrains what the AI can generate —
> treat entries as immutable once finalized.

---

## 1. Style & Voice Analysis

> Document the author's (your) prose patterns so AI assistants can match them.
> Be specific: vague descriptions like "lyrical" are less useful than concrete
> observations about sentence structure and rhythm.

### Sentence Rhythms
<!-- Examples:
- Average sentence length: 12-18 words, with periodic long compounds
- Favors tricolon (groups of three): "He walked, he waited, he wondered."
- Sentence fragments used for emphasis after long passages
-->

### POV Conventions
<!-- Examples:
- First person past tense throughout
- Unreliable narrator: protagonist omits details he finds shameful
- Frame story uses present tense; inner story uses past tense
-->

### Vocabulary & Register
<!-- Examples:
- Anglo-Saxon roots preferred over Latinate ("gut" not "intuition")
- Technical terms for magic system used precisely, never metaphorically
- Dialogue contractions always; narration contractions sometimes
-->

### Prose Patterns to Maintain
<!-- Examples:
- ALL CAPS for opening lines of chapters
- Scene breaks marked with ---
- Songs/poems in blockquotes, italicized
- Letters indented as blockquotes
-->

### Patterns to Avoid
<!-- Examples:
- No adverbs in dialogue tags ("said quietly" → "whispered")
- Never use "suddenly" — show the interruption instead
- Avoid filtering verbs: "he saw the door open" → "the door opened"
-->
