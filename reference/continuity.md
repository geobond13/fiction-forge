# Continuity Tracking

> **Purpose**: This file tracks facts that must remain consistent across the
> manuscript. AI continuity-checking tools scan this file alongside the story
> bible and foreshadowing ledger to flag contradictions.

---

## What to Track Here

Continuity errors are the most damaging mistakes in long fiction. Track:

- **Character states**: Injuries, emotional shifts, knowledge gained. If a
  character breaks their arm in Chapter 12, they cannot swing a sword in
  Chapter 13 without explanation.

- **Object locations**: Where important items are at any given point. If a
  letter is left on a desk in Chapter 8, it cannot be in a pocket in Chapter 9.

- **Timeline**: Days elapsed, seasons, travel times. If it takes three days to
  ride between two cities, characters cannot arrive the same afternoon.

- **Information flow**: What each character knows and when they learned it. A
  character cannot react to news they have not received.

- **World rules**: If your magic system has limits, they must be respected
  consistently. If a rule is broken, it must be a deliberate plot point.

## Instructions

1. Add entries as you write. Do not wait for a review pass.
2. The **Valid Through** column marks the last chapter where a fact still holds.
   Leave blank if it holds through the end of the manuscript.
3. AI continuity tools (`check_continuity`) validate chapter text against this
   file. Keep entries factual and specific.
